Goal

This narrative visualization presents a quest to find the shortest heighted rebounder in the game of basketball during 2016-2017 regular NBA season. It follows a vertically scrollable interactive slideshow structure, starting with an overview mix of all 486 players that played in the season. As viewer scrolls down the slides transition to the next scenes on the right section of the page with description on the left. When viewer reaches at the end of the page the story concludes.



Scenes

As viewer scrolls down, the scene opens by displaying a mix of all 486 players that played the 2016-2017 NBA season. One scene is transitioned to the next via vertical scroll event. In each of the scenes, the overview scene is updated as described in the visualization thereby maintaining a visual consistancy.

The scene template consists of a left panel taking about 25% of the space, and a right panel taking 75% of the screen space. The vertically scrolling left section displays a narrative text about the visualization being updated on the right side. The template provides a consistent navigation experience that is the key element to convey the intended information.



Annotations

Charts within the scenes use static annotations that bring attention to the user on some interesting facts displayed on it. For example, chart that displays "the tallest players colleting more rebouts per game" uses annotations to display tall player names. The concluding scene also displayes player's pic to annotate information.



Parameters

Visualization uses a unique verticle scroll experience. The screen is divided into different verticle scroll sections that work as a parameter. As viewer reaches a particular scroll section, corrosponding chart on the right is updated and filters certain data or adds new annotations etc.

For example, after overview scene, as user scrolls down to see the next scene, the scrolled section is used as a parameter to filter data, add new annotations to provide a consistent user experience as described by the chart description.

Triggers

Verticle scroll is the main trigger event that changes parameters, updates annotations and transitions scene from one narrative to the next. For example, when user scrolls down from introductory overview scene to the next scroll section, scroll event is triggered and parameters like player id , player name are passed to filter the data and update the chart on the screen.

Overview

In the game of basketball, the sharp shooters and double-figure scorers are the main attraction of the game. Another important aspect of the game is "rebounds".

Every team is relying on those who are able to get a lot of rebounds - in order to renew the clock shot after a missed shot or prevent from the opponent having a second chance to score.

Naturally the tallest players in the forward and center positions are responsible for grabbing the most rebounds for their teams. But there is always an occasional shortie with an aptitude for rebounds.

Looking at NBA stats for the past Regular Season (2016-2017), this visualization tries to indentify who was the shortest player to get the most rebounds last year.


Lets start by throwing in our mix all 486 players that played the 2016-2017 NBA Regular Season.


(keep scrolling down)

NBA stats database doesn't offer position information for a handful of players. Let's remove them from our final dataset.


Also, to consider only those who had a significant contribution to their team's game, Let's keep only those who played 12 minutes and over (that is at least one full quarter) per game.


Now let's arrange our 360 remaining NBA players according to their Height and RPG (Rebounds per game).


Rebound skills are equally distributed between the East and the West Conferences.


On the other hand, the Southeastern, the Pacific and the Northwestern Divisions have the top-3 rebounders, while the Atlantic and the Central Divisions have the two tallest players.


Naturally, the tallest players are collecting more rebounds per game.


And there is of course Russell Westbrook, who at 6'3" he grabbed 10.7 rebounds per game last year in over 34 minutes per game in 81 games with Oklahoma City Thunders. We will not include him in our quest for the shortest rebounder, because... he's Russell Westbrook, he's an outlier by his own right.


Therefore this guy is the shortest player who got the most rebounds per game. He's Avery Bradley and last year he played for the Boston Celtics. With 6'4" height, he got 6.1 rebounds per game in 55 games played. This year he plays for the Detroit Pistons where he only receives 2.4 RPG in 28 Games Played (as of December 16).


Avery however wasn't always the rebounder kind of guy. Actually his 2016-2017 6.1 RPG was a career high, near to double from his previous record of 2013-2014 season with 3.8 Rebounds per Game.
